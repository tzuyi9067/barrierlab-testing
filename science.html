Chapter 01

What is eczema, actually?

Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is a chronic condition where the skin barrier lets water out and irritants in, while the immune system overreacts to things that shouldn't matter. It's itchy, it flares, and roughly 1 in 10 Americans live with some form of it. For most people, it has two drivers: a compromised skin barrier on the outside, and an overactive immune signal that often starts in the gut.

Healthy skin vs flaring skin

In flaring skin, the outer layer is missing ceramides and cracks between cells. Water evaporates, irritants get in, and nerves fire itch signals. The immune system, already primed, overreacts.
